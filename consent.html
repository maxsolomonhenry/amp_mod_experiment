REB 162-0817
Dismantling the illusion of amplitude modulation-induced vibrato

 

WHY ARE WE DOING THIS RESEARCH? Our aim is to establish scientific knowledge on the role of amplitude modulation rate and resonant structure on the perception of vibrato in musical sounds.

 

PRIVACY. We know that you value your privacy. All data will be held securely in a password-protected computer system. Only the principal investigator of the study and participating students will have access to your Prolific ID. You may discontinue your participation in this study at any time either during the experiment or in the future.

 

WHAT WILL HAPPEN DURING THE EXPERIMENT? You have just been shown the necessary conditions for participating in this experiment (you have normal hearing and are in a quiet room, wearing headphones and doing the experiment from a laptop or desktop computer, not a cell phone or tablet, and you are not currently taking medical or recreational drugs that affect wakefulness and vigilance). If you cannot accommodate these conditions, we ask that you withdraw from the experiment. If you accept these conditions, example sounds will be presented so that you can set the volume to a comfortable level that still allows you to hear the softest sounds. You will go through the instructions of the experiment and fill out the first questionnaire. Then you will run through a short practice block to get familiar with the listening task and will then continue on to the listening experiment. (It's important you hear all the music at the same level, so please do not change the level during the experiment.) The actual listening test will have 88 trials divided into four blocks. Upon completion of the final trial, you will return to Prolific. The experiment will take approximately eight minutes. You will be compensated £1 by Prolific for your participation. We are interested in how people in general respond to these stimuli.

 

The only identifiable information collected will be your Prolific ID. This ID will be disassociated from your data in approximately 6 months. If you choose to withdraw during the study, all information obtained until that point will be deleted and you will receive partial payment through Prolific. If you choose to withdraw after completing the study, you need to contact the researcher with your Prolific ID before the moment when the data are dissociated from the Prolific IDs.

 

This research is funded by the Canadian Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council. The Research Ethics Board II of McGill University has reviewed this study for compliance with ethical standards.
